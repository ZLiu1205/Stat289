Bibimbap

Bibimbap [2] ( / ˈ b iː b ɪ m b æ p / BEE -bim-bap , [3] from Korean bibimbap [pi.bim.p͈ap̚] , literally "mixed rice"), sometimes romanized as bi bim bap or bi bim bop , is a Korean rice dish. Bibimbap is served as a bowl of warm white rice topped with namul (sautéed and seasoned vegetables) and gochujang ( chili pepper paste), soy sauce, or doenjang (a fermented soybean paste). A raw or fried egg and sliced meat (usually beef ) are common additions. The hot dish is stirred together thoroughly just before eating. [4]

Bibimbap is a popular Korean dish . The word "bibimbap" literally means "mixed rice " or "mixed meal." Bibimbap is rice topped with vegetables , beef , and egg , and mixed with chilli pepper paste. A variation of this dish, "dolsot bibimbap" (돌솥 비빔밥) is served in a heated stone bowl, in which a raw egg is cooked against the sides of the bowl.
