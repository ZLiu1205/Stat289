Chazuke (茶漬け) or Ochazuke (お茶漬け, from o + cha tea + zuke ) is a simple Japanese dish made by pouring boiling green tea or water over rice roughly in the same proportion as milk over cereal . It usually has a topping. Common toppings include pickled vegetables , umeboshi , nori (seaweed), furikake , sesame seeds , salted cod roe , salted salmon , sea bream , soy and wasabi .

A very simple Japanese dish made with green tea poared over a bowl of plain white rice , roughly in the same proportion as milk over cereal . Often it is served topped with wasabi , soy , pickled vegetables , fish (raw or cooked), or nori seaweed. The dish is easy to make and provides a great way to use leftover rice as a quick snack.
