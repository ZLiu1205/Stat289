Gujeolpan

Gujeolpan refers to either an elaborate Korean dish consisting of nine different foods assorted on a wooden plate with nine divided sections in an octagon shape or the plate itself. The name is composed of three hanja words: gu (구, "nine" ), jeol (절, "section"), and pan (판, "plate") in Korean . Foods are separated by color and ingredients, and comprise various namul (seasoned leaf vegetables ), meats, mushrooms, and seafood items. In the center of the tray is a stack of small jeon (Korean style pancakes ) made with wheat flour , which are called miljeonbyeong (밀전병). In addition to its use as a food platter used to serve many dishes of food at once, gujeolpan is also considered a decorative item. [1] [2] [3]
