Oyakodon

Oyakodon ( 親子丼 ) , literally "parent-and-child donburi", is a donburi , or Japanese rice bowl dish, in which chicken, egg, sliced scallion (or sometimes regular onions), and other ingredients are all simmered together in a kind of soup which is made with soy sauce and stock, and then served on top of a large bowl of rice. The name of the dish is a poetic reflection of the fact that both chicken and egg are used in the dish. [1]

Oyakodon ( 親子丼 ) , literally "parent-and-child donburi", is a donburi , or Japanese rice bowl dish , in which chicken , egg , green onions , and other ingredients are all simmered together in a sauce and then served on top of a large bowl of rice . The name of the dish, parent and child donburi , is a poetic reflection of the fact that both chicken and egg are used in the dish. [note 1] In Japan , oyakodon is often served in soba restaurants and other traditional Japanese restaurants.
