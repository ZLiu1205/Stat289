Butajiru

Tonjiru ( 豚汁,とんじる ) or Butajiru ( 豚汁,ぶたじる ) — both literally mean pig/pork soup — is a Japanese soup made with pork and vegetables, flavoured with miso .

Tonjiru or Butajiru (豚汁) - both literally mean pig/pork soup - is a Japanese soup with made with pork and miso paste. Mildly degreased (not crispy) bacon can be used in place of pork. Compared to usual miso soup , the flavor of pork harmonizes with wide variety of other ingredients with harshness such as gobo and konjac that dominate the taste in case of miso soup recipe. On the contrary, ingredients with delicate taste become unclear in tonjiru recipe. Common ingredients may include gobo , konjac , seaweed , spring onions , daikon radish, carrot , tofu including fried tofu ( aburaage ), potatoes including taro and sweet potato , mushrooms such as shiitake and shimeji , and other vegetables. It is often eaten after marathons and other long distance races.
